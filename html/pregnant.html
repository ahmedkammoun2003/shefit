Recommendations Related to Physical Activity

The latest Canadian recommendations for physical activity during pregnancy indicate that, to maximize the benefits of physical activity, pregnant women should:

  • engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity per week.

    • During moderate-intensity activity, the person should be able to talk, but not sing. The effort should be enough to significantly increase their heart rate (brisk walking, stationary cycling, aqua aerobics, snowshoeing, etc.);

  • move at least 3 times per week,

    • but preferably every day;

  • vary between cardiovascular and muscular activities;

    • perform pelvic strengthening exercises

    every day (e.g., Kegel exercises). For optimal results, it is recommended to learn the correct execution technique of the exercises.

The Benefits of Physical Activity

If your doctor or midwife gives you the green light, go for it! Here are some of the benefits demonstrated by research that you will experience if you engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity per week:

  • a 32% reduction in the risk of excessive weight gain during pregnancy;
  • a 38% reduction in the risk of gestational diabetes;
  • a 40% reduction in the risks of high blood pressure and preeclampsia;
  • better physical fitness, resulting in reduced shortness of breath;
  • at least a 25% reduction in the risk of depression;
  • improved self-esteem;
  • a decrease in the severity of back pain;
  • a decrease in the risk and severity of urinary incontinence symptoms during pregnancy;
  • a reduction in medical interventions during childbirth;
  • a reduction in the risk of having a large baby (weighing more than 4 kg) at birth.

Studies have shown that moderate-intensity exercise does not increase the risk of miscarriage, preterm birth, low birth weight baby, or cesarean section.
