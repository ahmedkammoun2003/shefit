The Dish

This recipe is partly inspired by the popular English breakfast, with just a slight nutritional modification and simplification for quick and easy preparation, whether it's on busy weekdays or weekends. Homemade turkey sausage patties – well-seasoned with sage, thyme, and paprika – are pan-seared and served with a sunny-side-up egg. A pan-seared tomato and toasted whole wheat bread complete this morning meal. If you're still hungry, feel free to serve this hearty meal with a fruit salad or a warm bowl of cereal.

Hack this Recipe

This turkey sausage recipe can easily be prepared a day or two in advance, either with the raw seasoned mixture or with the pan-cooked and cooled sausage. You can also freeze the cooked sausage, thaw it, and reheat it when you're ready to eat.

Preparation Instructions

Lightly grease a medium non-stick skillet with non-stick cooking spray and heat over medium-high heat.
Place the ground turkey in a medium bowl and season with garlic powder, sage, thyme, paprika, and a small pinch of salt. Using your hands, mix to combine and divide into 2 patties.
Once the skillet is hot, add the turkey sausage patties and cook for about 2 minutes on each side, or until cooked through. Remove the sausage patties from the skillet, reserve them on a serving plate, and lightly grease the skillet with additional cooking spray.
Add the egg and halved tomato, cut side down, to the skillet; reduce heat to medium-low and cover. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until the egg is cooked to your liking and the tomato is tender.
Lightly season both with salt and pepper and transfer them to the plate with the sausage.
Finally, cut the whole wheat toast into quarters and add them to the plate before serving.
